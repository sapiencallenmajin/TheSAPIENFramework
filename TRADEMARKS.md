# SAPIEN Trademark & Brand Usage Policy

> **Template — review with counsel before publishing.** The brand is *one*
> durable layer of the moat (alongside your private corpus/calibration data and
> shipping velocity) — not the whole thing. "SAPIEN" will likely register as a
> relatively *thin* mark in a crowded field, so treat it as important but not
> load-bearing. The methodology is free to copy; the *name* is what you control.
> Model: the same approach MITRE uses for ATT&CK® (free framework, strictly
> controlled mark).

## 1. Marks owned by SAPIEN Labs LLC

| Mark | Type | Use |
|---|---|---|
| **SAPIEN™** | Word mark | The framework, project, and product family |
| **SAPIEN Score™ / SAPIEN Rating™** | Word marks | The numeric outputs of an assessment |
| **SAPIEN Certified℠** | **Certification mark** | Only for implementations/organizations we have certified |
| Logo / wordmark | Design mark | The SAPIEN visual identity |

Until registrations issue, use ™ (goods/brands) and ℠ (the certification
service). Do **not** use ® until a federal registration is granted. Note:
"SAPIEN" is close to a dictionary term, so expect possible descriptiveness /
crowded-field objections at the USPTO — file early, and strengthen the position
with a distinctive logo and composite marks (e.g., "SAPIEN SCORE").

> **Note on "Voigt-Kampff":** this name alludes to a third party's
> copyrighted work and is **not** a SAPIEN-owned mark. Keep it as an internal
> code alias only. Do not build commercial brand equity on it and do not seek
> to register it.

## 2. What anyone MAY do (nominative / descriptive use)

- State that their tool **"implements the SAPIEN Framework"** or is
  **"compatible with the SAPIEN Framework v1.x."**
- Reference SAPIEN dimensions, scores, and the spec when citing the methodology
  (CC BY 4.0 attribution applies to the text of the spec).
- Use the marks in plain text to refer to *our* project (not theirs).

## 3. What requires our written permission

- Using **SAPIEN** (or any confusingly similar term) **in a product, service,
  company, or domain name.**
- Stating or implying that a product, model, or organization is
  **"SAPIEN Certified,"** holds an official **SAPIEN Rating**, or is endorsed,
  sponsored, or affiliated with SAPIEN Labs LLC.
- Using the SAPIEN logo, or any modified form of it.
- Using the marks in a way likely to cause confusion as to origin.

## 4. The "SAPIEN Certified" program

"SAPIEN Certified℠" may be applied **only** to implementations or organizations
that meet the published SAPIEN conformance standard and have been certified by
SAPIEN Labs LLC (or an authorized body) under a written certification agreement.
A certification mark carries legal control duties for the owner (see
*15 U.S.C. § 1064(5)*) — the program standard, the application process, and the
certifier's role must be set up with counsel **before** the mark is used in
commerce.

> **Critical:** a certification-mark owner generally may **not** also sell the
> goods/services it certifies. SAPIEN Labs LLC / Synthreo must **not** place
> "SAPIEN Certified" on their own scanner, reports, or services — the mark
> certifies *third-party* implementations and models. Brand Synthreo's own
> offerings with the ordinary **SAPIEN™** trademark. **Decide this structure in
> Phase 0:** running "SAPIEN Certified" from the same entity that sells the
> scanner risks cancellation and discriminatory-administration challenges, so
> default to an **independent certifying entity** with a neutral published
> standard and non-discriminatory access.
